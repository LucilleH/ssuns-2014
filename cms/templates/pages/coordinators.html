Peter Laing

Page Co-Coordinator

Born in Johannesburg, South Africa, Peter made stops in Perth, Australia and London, England before completing his commonwealth tour and finally settling in the quiet town of Oakville, Ontario. An economics major in his third year at McGill, Peter had his first taste of the international realm after violating ICAO (International Civil Aviation Organization) regulation at the age of nine. The naive youngster forgot a souvenir Swiss Army knife in his pocket before boarding an international flight and was subsequently detained for 6 hours. After being told participating in Model United Nations would get him into university, Peter used his time in the brig to successfully land a spot in his high school’s Model United Nations club. An unforgettable experience at NAIMUN in Washington D.C. led Peter to fall in love with current events and become a self-described news junkie. Admittedly, opting to read his subscription to ESPN before that of The Economist, Peter is nevertheless ready to kick some MUN Djibouti. When he isn’t telling a bad joke, Peter can be found getting too intense in intramural volleyball, drinking green tea or enjoying the company of the abundance of awesome people that inhabit the island of Montreal.

Speaking of exceptional individuals, Peter is excited to have the opportunity to work with such a talented and important group of pages this year. Being the lifeblood of the conference, Peter looks forward to working alongside the conference’s paging staff to ensure an amazing and jail free time for all at McMUN 2013.
